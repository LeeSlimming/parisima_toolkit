Job Descriptions

WHAT?

A Job Description is exactly that – a description of the job. A good job description should outline the general activities and responsibilities of the job and is used to help applicants determine whether the role is in line with their skillset and experience. If being used externally, a job description should also include a brief description of the company, global presence, nature of the industry and any other information angled at attracting a candidate.

WHY?

If used correctly, a Job Description can be an effective marketing tool and should be shared with both the recruiter and candidate to open opportunities for questions. They are written in partnership with the Hiring Manager and Human Resource/Talent Acquisition. Job Description should not be shared internally or externally without being reviewed and approved via a pre-set governance framework.

WHEN?

A good recruiter will always ask for a job description at the very start of the recruitment process but by no means should it be used as the only source of information to begin a search – you will fail! The Job Description is a starting point for the recruiter to ask the hiring manager further questions and at the same time, a commitment from the hiring manager to make them think about the type of profile they are looking for.

In the next phase of the recruitment process, the Role Briefing conversation, the recruiter get the opportunity to discover further information about the candidate profile and the essential competencies to help tailor their search (see job briefing section).

HOW?

Experienced recruiters will know that in most cases, job descriptions are not updated or, simply do not exist which can often add a challenge when trying to entice candidates, not to mention, not do much for the company perception in the market. If you want to get the hiring manager thinking about his /her needs and at the same time, sell the role and the company to candidates – why not write it yourself?

Hiring great talent starts with attracting great talent. In this section, we will walk you through creating effective, engaging and inclusive job descriptions targeted to the best candidates for the job.
